I began acting at the age of 16. I started at Walnut High School where I was introduced to Theater in my junior year. I started taking acting more seriously in class, and decided to audition for shows. I got cast in many high school productions, and continued to gain more experience in acting as I pursued it. In senior year of high school, I auditioned to get into the Advanced Theater class and got a spot. From there, I decided I'd major in acting, since I had a huge passion for it. From my highschool experience I learned break out of my shell, appreciate acting, and all the hard work that's put into a production.

In college I continued to work on productions. It was here that I met friends who were just as passionate about acting and entertainment, we started creating and writing our own skits and would sometimes travel to record them on our breaks from school. This taught me the importance of story flow, contiguity in our work, and teamwork. This helped me to learn more about filming and acting on the camera alongside the numerous acting related courses I took at Mt. San Antonio College prior to transferring to UCR.

More recently, I started to find work for acting. I was successful in getting into Central Casting, an agency where I'm often contacted for available acting opportunities. This gave me a taste of what professional acting was like, I was getting paid to act all while interacting with big names in the industry. This further motivated me to continue my journey as a storyteller and so I decided to educate myself more at University of California, Riverside. Here I am learning and honing my acting skills as well as networking to further increase my chances at a successful acting career.
